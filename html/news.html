Rain Continues

Published May 01, 2000

Today marks the fourth straight month of intense flooding across Alberta and northern Montana, and most of Hartshorn Creek has now been evacuated.

RCMP are aiding efforts to locate remaining residents after the body of Marge Deckland was discovered by police in her basement last Wednesday. She was featured in our obituary section.

“It’s urgent people find a way to higher ground,” said RCMP officer Marsh, “whether that be the facilities available in Banff and Lake Louise or South into Waterton.”

Despite coordinated efforts, long processing times and slowdowns at the US border have left many still stranded on highways, though Marsh again urged against attempting shortcuts on backroads.

The statue of town founder Emmet Elken is completely submerged now. Some have said that it seemed to be waving goodbye to passing search boats with one outstretched hand still visible above the waterline, while other reports have claimed that the statue had mysteriously disappeared. A fitting final mystery for our once-quiet town known for its outsized share of urban legends.

We who remain at the Gazette intend to document all further developments in this story. We’re not giving up our journalistic mission to serve our community, and we hope you continue to tune in for the latest updates on the situation and that you and your family are able to stay safe.
